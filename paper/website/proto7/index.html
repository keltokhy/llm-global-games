Level 1

Your Briefing

You're a citizen in an authoritarian country. An intelligence briefing has arrived describing the regime's current state. Read it carefully — then decide.

What do you do?

If enough citizens JOIN and the attack mass exceeds regime strength θ, the regime falls.
If not enough join, the regime survives — and joiners are punished.

Interesting, right? But one round tells you nothing.

The S-curve! From 45,000 individual binary choices, a smooth sigmoid emerges — matching the theoretical prediction with r = +0.80 (p < 0.001). Each AI found the same threshold strategy independently.

But is it really the signal driving behavior, or just sentiment?

Conclusion

What We Learned

AI agents don't just parrot text. They play strategic games with sophistication that matches economic theory.

r = +0.80

They follow theory

Across 7 models and 45,000 decisions, join rates track the predicted sigmoid with high correlation.

r ≈ 0

Scramble kills it

Randomly reassigning briefings destroys the correlation. It's the signal, not the text sentiment.

r = −0.72

Flip inverts it

Negate the signal and behavior inverts. Agents process information, not vibes.

r = +0.97

Payoffs matter

Varying benefit/cost narratives shifts thresholds exactly as theory predicts — a 50pp swing invisible to text classifiers.

−13.5pp

Surveillance chills

Expressed joining falls by ~13.5 percentage points under surveillance. Beliefs barely move (<1pp).

0pp

Propaganda saturates

Doubling propaganda plants from 5 to 10 adds zero additional effect. The channel has finite capacity.
